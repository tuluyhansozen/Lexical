The Power of Accidental Discovery

History is replete with tales of scientists and explorers stumbling upon ground-breaking discoveries while searching for something entirely different. This phenomenon, often described as serendipity, plays a crucial role in the advancement of human knowledge.

Consider Alexander Fleming, who returned from a holiday to find a petri dish colonized by mold. Instead of discarding it, he examined the anomaly and discovered penicillin.

This moment wasn't just luck; it was prepared observation meeting an unexpected event. In literature and daily life, we encounter similar moments.
